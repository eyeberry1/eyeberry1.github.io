FDTD: finite-difference time -domain method

Maxwell's equations:

等我学完再写(先挖个坑)

Discretize the fields in time and space

An important parameter: point per wavelength

Defined by"Mesh arruracy",ranging from 1-10.Usually set to 2

The frequency domain response is calculated by Fourier transform. Many quantities are derived from the frequency domain fields, such as the Poynting vector, transmission and reflection spectrum, the field power profile,etc.

frequency domain: 频域

Poynting vector: 坡印亭矢量

field power profile: 场强分布

Essential components of FDTD simulation:

Structure(几何结构)

Material(材料)

Source(光源)

Simulation region(仿真区域)

Monitor(监视器)

Sorce

  1. Dipole source: utilized as point source

    (偶极子光源,用于仿真点光源)

  2. Plane wave: ideal source with infinite size

    (平面波,大小无限大,通常用于周期性结构)

  3. TPSF(total-field scatter-field source):

    1. inside the source region

    -incident light(入射光) and scattering light(散射光)

    1. outside the source region

    -only scatter light

    (粉色箭头表示入射光入射方向,蓝色箭头表示电场振动方向)

    (白色区域内包含入射场和散射场,白色区域外只有散射场,方便计算)

  4. Gaussian source: represnts light from laser source(激光器) or light from anobjective lens(物镜) with a given NA

    (nonperiodic) (Grating Coupler)

  5. Mode source: represents eigen modes (e.g. TE/TM) in waveguides(表示波导中的本征模式)

    补充:

Simulation region

Boundary condition

  1. PML: perfectly matched layer,

    100% absorption, 0% reflection

  2. Metal: perfect electric conductor (PEC)

    100% reflection, 0% absorption

    (usually used when there is nearly no light at the boundaries of simulation region, because it takes the shortest time to simulate using Metal boundaries)

  3. Periodic boundaries: used when the structures and field prolife are periodic ,for vertical incident light

  4. Bloch boundaries: similar to periodic boundaries,

    but for angled incident light

    (periodic/bloch boundaries work with plane wave)

  5. Symmetric/anti-symmetric boundaries: used when the structures and field are symmetric or anti-symmetric (e.g. TE mode, positive electric field against negative electric field)

tips

A tip for set-up: apply symmetric conditions to the boundaries which are parallel to theblue arrow of light (representing the electric field)
